Main Character

Life is Strange

Max Caulfield

"I WISH I COULD STAY IN THIS MOMENT FOREVER."

Maxine "Max" Caulfield is an 18-year-old photography student who discovers she can rewind time after witnessing the death of her childhood best friend Chloe Price. Returning to her hometown of Arcadia Bay, Oregon, Max must navigate friendships, dark secrets, and impossible choices—all while a devastating storm approaches that threatens to destroy everything she loves.

The Rewind

It begins in a bathroom. A gunshot. Chloe falls. Max reaches out—and time reverses. She can rewind moments, undo mistakes, explore every possibility.

But every power has a price.

Chloe Price

Blue hair, punk attitude, broken heart. Max's former best friend, abandoned five years ago when Max moved to Seattle without looking back.

Chloe's father died. Her mother remarried. Her friend Rachel vanished. And Max wasn't there for any of it.

Now fate—or Max's power—keeps saving Chloe from death. Again and again.

Blackwell Academy

A prestigious arts school in Arcadia Bay. Home to dreamers, artists, and dark secrets hidden behind perfect smiles.

Rachel Amber

Missing: Six months ago

  • The girl everyone loved
  • Chloe's closest friend after Max left
  • Her disappearance haunts the town
  • Missing person posters everywhere

What happened to Rachel Amber?

The Storm

Max sees it in her visions. A tornado of impossible size bearing down on Arcadia Bay. Friday. This Friday. The town will be destroyed.

Is her power causing it? Can she stop it? Does saving Chloe doom everyone else?

Through the Lens

Max sees the world through her camera. Polaroids capture moments that can never be changed—or can they?

Every Choice Matters

  • Save Kate or let her fall?
  • Expose Nathan or protect Chloe?
  • Trust Warren or go alone?
  • Tell the truth or rewind the lie?
Time can be rewound. Consequences cannot.

The Vortex Club

Blackwell's elite. Rich kids, wild parties, dangerous secrets. Nathan Prescott and Victoria Chase rule this world.

Kate Marsh

A quiet, religious girl destroyed by a viral video from a Vortex Club party. She doesn't remember what happened that night.

She stands on the roof of the dormitory. Max's power fails. Only her words can save Kate now.

This moment cannot be rewound.

Nathan Prescott

Son of the wealthiest family in Arcadia Bay. Unstable. Dangerous. Protected by money and power.

He shot Chloe in that bathroom. He drugged Kate. But he's also a victim—manipulated by someone far worse.

The Dark Room

A photography bunker hidden beneath the Prescott barn. Women drugged and photographed in their most vulnerable moments. Binders full of victims.

Rachel Amber's final photograph is here. So is her body.

Mark Jefferson—beloved teacher, famous artist—is the monster behind the lens.

The Photograph

Max discovers she can jump into photographs—traveling back years, not seconds. She saves Chloe's father from dying.

In this timeline, Chloe is paralyzed. She asks Max to end her suffering. The butterfly effect is cruel.

Warren Graham

Science nerd. Loyal friend. Hopelessly in love with Max. Always there when she needs him—even when she doesn't notice.

The Truth

Every time Max saves Chloe, she tears reality. The storm isn't random—it's the universe correcting itself. Chloe was meant to die in that bathroom.

To save Arcadia Bay, Max must let her die.

To save Chloe, Max must let everyone else die.

There is no rewind for this choice.

Two Endings

Sacrifice Chloe: Max travels back to the bathroom. Watches Chloe die. Attends her funeral alone. Arcadia Bay survives. Their week together—erased.

Sacrifice Arcadia Bay: Max tears the photograph. The storm destroys the town. Everyone they knew may be dead. But Chloe lives. They drive away together into an uncertain future.

Summary

Life is Strange asks impossible questions about love, sacrifice, and whether we can truly change fate. Max's journey isn't about powers—it's about connection, grief, and learning that some moments can't be rewound. The only choice that matters is how much we're willing to sacrifice for the people we love.

"Wherever I end up after this... in whatever reality... all those moments between us were real, and they'll always be ours." — Max Caulfield
